# **Relative Volume (RVOL)**

**Relative Volume (RVOL)** in stocks measures current trading activity compared to a stock's average volume over a specific period (e.g., 20 or 50 days). Calculated as **Current Volume / Average  Volume**, a value (>1.0) indicates higher-than-usual interest (potential breakouts/reversals), while ==(<1.0) suggests low interest==. It helps identify liquidity and confirms strength in price moves.

## **Key Details About Relative Volume**

* **Interpretation:** An RVOL of 2.0 means the stock is trading twice its typical volume, suggesting significant market attention.
* **Common Thresholds:** Values above ==1.5 or 2.0 are often used by traders== to identify stocks with unusual activity or high volatility.
* **Purpose:** It acts as a confirmation tool for trends; high volume validates price trends, while low volume can indicate a weak move or impending reversal.
* **Benefits:** Increased relative volume indicates higher liquidity, which results in tighter spreads and less slippage for traders.

RVOL is generally used in conjunction with other technical indicators to analyze market sentiment and identify trading opportunities.
